Tonkotsu Ramen


This recipes prepares a delicious bowl of Tonkotsu Ramen made with pork bone broth. Top the ramen with pork belly, green onions, and a soft boiled egg results in a comforting and satisfying meal that is perfect for a cold day.

Ingredients

  • 2 pounds pork neck bones
  • 1 pound pork belly
  • 1 onion
  • 1 head of garlic
  • 1 piece of ginger
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup mirin
  • 1/4 cup sake
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup miso paste
  • 1/4 cup sesame oil
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup green onions
  • 1/4 cup bean sprouts
  • 1/4 cup corn
  • 1/4 cup bamboo shoots
  • 1/4 cup seaweed
  • 1/4 cup soft boiled eggs
  • 1/4 cup ramen noodles

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 450 degrees F.
  2. Place the pork neck bones on a baking sheet and roast in the oven for 1 hour.
  3. Place the pork belly in a large pot and cover with water.
  4. Bring to a boil and simmer for 1 hour.
  5. Remove the pork belly from the pot and set aside.
  6. Place the onion, garlic, and ginger in the pot and cook until soft.
  7. Add the roasted pork neck bones to the pot and cover with water.
  8. Bring to a boil and simmer for 12 hours.
  9. Strain the broth and discard the solids.
  10. Return the broth to the pot and add the soy sauce, mirin, sake, sugar, and miso paste.
  11. Bring to a boil and simmer for 1 hour.
  12. Heat the sesame oil and vegetable oil in a pan.
  13. Add the green onions, bean sprouts, corn, bamboo shoots, and seaweed to the pan and cook until soft.
  14. Cook the ramen noodles according to the package instructions.
  15. Place the noodles in a bowl and top with the pork belly, vegetables, and soft boiled egg.
  16. Pour the broth over the top and serve hot.
